Type of Patent

  • Utility Patents

    A utility patent is a detailed document that explains how to use a new machine, process, or system and is what most people typically associate with patents. Defines the types of inventions that can receive utility patent protection, but advancements in technology, such as genetic engineering and internet-delivered software, are pushing the limits of what can be protected.

  • Provisional patent

    A provisional patent is a more informal document that demonstrates that the inventor had the invention in their possession and understood how to make it function properly. This filing date establishes the invention as patent pending. However, the inventor can lose this filing date if a formal utility patent is not submitted within a year following the provisional patent's filing.

  • Design Patent

    A design patent protects an object's ornamental design, such as the bottle's shape or the shoes' style. The document mainly consists of pictures or drawings of the design, making it challenging to search. In recent years, software companies have utilized design patents to protect aspects of user interfaces and even the shape of touchscreen devices.

  • Plant Patent

    A plant patent protects new types of plants that are produced through nonsexual means, such as cuttings. However, genetically modified organisms are not covered under plant patents, which primarily focus on conventional horticulture.
