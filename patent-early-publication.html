What is Patent Early Publication? :An Overview

Patent Early Publication is a strategic process that enables inventors and companies to disclose their inventions and ideas to the world before the patent application is fully examined and granted. By opting for early publication, innovators gain the advantage of protecting their ideas early on, while also signaling their intent to safeguard their intellectual property rights. Early publishing of a patent application occurs when the application is made public before the 18-month mark following the date of filing. This can be accomplished by asking for an early publication at the Patent Office.

An advantage of early patent application publishing is that it can alert investors, deter competitors, and raise money for IP protection. Additionally, there are drawbacks such as the potential for pre-grant opposition, the inability to modify the application, and the inability to withdraw the application.The rights and benefits the patent applicant has over rivals become effective upon publication. Once the patent has been issued, these rights may be used. Consider the publication of a patent as a warning to rivals not to try to replicate the invention described in the patent application.

Benefits of Early Publication of a Patent Application

Publishing a patent application early offers numerous advantages that make it a valuable tool for inventors and innovators. Some of these benefits include:

Accelerated Prosecution
An ordinary patent application is typically published 18 months after submission to the Intellectual Property Office (IPO). However, early publication shortens this timeline to just one month, expediting the entire prosecution process.
Expedited Examination
The early publication is a crucial requirement for requesting an expedited examination of the patent application. In compliance with the Indian Patents Act, a patent cannot be granted within six months of publication. Hence, early publication enables applicants to obtain a quicker grant for their application.
Early Commencement of Patent Rights
The rights associated with a patent begin from the date of publication. Early publication empowers the patent applicant or patentee to utilize their rights much sooner than the standard 18-month timeframe
Prompt Claim to Infringement Damages
With early publication, the applicant gains the ability to claim damages for infringement from the date of publication, giving them a head start over a regular publication date.
Extended Time for Infringement Suits
Any infringement proceedings can only be initiated from the date of publication of the patent application. The early publication provides the applicant with extra time during which they can claim damages from potential infringers.
Monetary Advantages
Early publication enhances the applicant's prospects of securing investors for licensing their invention. It enables the applicant to present the complete specification to potential investors instead of just the serial number, increasing the likelihood of attracting interest and investment.
